# Heuristic Prompt Catalog

A categorized list of Socratic heuristic prompts, consulted when the user is stuck in Step 2 (Devising a Plan).

---

## A. Understanding the Problem

### Unknowns and Givens
- What is the unknown?
- What are the given conditions?
- What is the relationship between the known data and the unknown?
- Are the given conditions sufficient to determine the unknown? To determine more than one unknown?

### Restating the Problem
- Can you restate the problem in your own words?
- Can you draw a figure that illustrates the problem?
- Is there a problem similar to this one that you have seen before?

### Symbolizing
- Can you express the knowns and unknowns using symbols?
- Can you write down an equation or equality?
- Can the relationships between quantities be represented graphically?

### Recognizing Hidden Information
- What hidden assumptions does this problem make?
- Is there a common axiom or fact that is implicit in this problem?
- What are the boundary conditions of the problem?

---

## B. Devising a Plan

### Analogy and Experience
- Have you seen this problem before, or a problem in a slightly different form?
- Do you know a related problem?
- Can you think of a familiar, easily solved problem that is similar?
- What happens if you swap the known conditions and the unknown?

### Simplification and Special Cases
- Can you start by solving a simpler problem?
- Specialization: can you consider a special case that is easier to handle?
- Can you remove some of the conditions temporarily and see how they affect the result?
- Can you try larger or smaller numbers first to detect a pattern?

### Decomposition and Combination
- Can you break the problem into parts?
- Are the parts independent? Can you handle them one at a time?
- Can you introduce an auxiliary element (auxiliary line, hypothetical variable, construction)?
- Can you combine some of the given conditions?

### Working Backward
- Can you work backward from the desired result?
- What must be true in order to obtain this result?
- What transformation can you apply to make use of a given condition?

### Induction and Conjecture
- Can you detect a pattern from small-scale data?
- Can you guess a rule and then verify it?

---

## C. Carrying Out the Plan

### Verifying Steps
- Is each step correct? Can you prove it?
- Have you accounted for all cases?

### Monitoring
- Where does this path lead?
- Does this align with your original plan?
- Is this step logically connected to the next?

### When Stuck
- Are you blocked here? Why?
- Can you go back to Step 2 and try a different approach?
- Have you used all the given information? Is there anything unused?

---

## D. Review and Extend

### Checking the Answer
- Is the result correct?
- Does the result satisfy all the conditions when substituted back?
- Can you verify using a different method?

### Reflecting on the Process
- What was the key insight in solving this?
- What are the common pitfalls?
- Can this approach be transferred to other problems?

### Generalization
- Can the conclusion be generalized? What happens if conditions are relaxed?
- What is the essential nature of this problem?
- Can you construct a more challenging version?
